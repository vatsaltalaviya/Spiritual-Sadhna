Sacred Transmission

Diksha & Discipleship

"Diksha is sacred transmission. It is not conferred casually."

Entry into the Himalayan Siddhashram Guru Parampara begins with preparation. Before initiation, the aspirant enters a structured preparatory phase designed to stabilise body, mind, and intention.

Foundation

PREPARATORY PERIOD — STAGE 0

This period establishes discipline, evaluates readiness, and aligns the seeker with the seriousness of Sādhana. Preparation is not optional. It is foundational.

Purpose

  • ● Establish daily rhythm of practice
  • ● Strengthen mental steadiness
  • ● Regulate lifestyle patterns
  • ● Cultivate humility and receptivity
  • ● Assess commitment to continuity

Duration

The preparatory phase typically extends for a minimum of 8–12 weeks. In some cases, it may continue longer based on individual readiness. Progression is determined by steadiness — not by time alone.

Daily Discipline

  • Fixed Daily Sitting Designated, uninterrupted time. Consistency is key.
  • Foundational Japa Prescribed universal mantra for focus and pronunciation.
  • Breath Regulation Simple pranayama to stabilise prāṇa.
  • Silent Sitting Quiet awareness following mantra repetition.
  • Practice Journal Daily record of duration, state, and observations.

Lifestyle & Conduct

Lifestyle Alignment

  • ✓ Regulated sleep schedule
  • ✓ Reduced sensory stimulation
  • ✓ Moderate/eliminate intoxicants
  • ✓ Adopt sattvic dietary discipline
  • ✓ Practice restraint in speech

Conduct Expectations

  • ✕ No extra mantras or rituals
  • ✕ No mixing of multiple systems
  • ✕ No public declaration of status
  • ✕ No pursuit of occult/power

Readiness

Aspirants must inform the Guru of significant psychological or neurological conditions. Stability is valued over intensity.

Avoid abandoning professional treatment for spiritual experimentation.

Evaluation for Diksha

Conducted at the conclusion of Stage 0

The Journey

SĀDHANA PROGRESSION

"Krama-Based Advancement Within the Parampara"

Krama means ordered unfolding. Just as a seed becomes a tree through natural stages, spiritual refinement develops through structured preparation. When stages are honored, transmission stabilises.

0

Preparation

Stabilising the instrument. Daily discipline and mental steadiness.

1

Foundational Diksha

Mantra discipline, Guru anchoring, and basic ritual structure.

2

Structured Devatā

Ganapati, Bala. Mantra begins to deepen into awareness.

3

Śrī Vidyā Krama

Progressive transmission only after demonstrated steadiness.

  • • Mahā Gaṇapati
  • • Guru Pādukā
  • • Bālā Tripura
  • • Mantriṇī
  • • Vārāhī
  • • Nityā Devīs
  • • Pañcadaśī
  • • Nava-Āvaraṇa
4

Integration

Extended Anushṭhāna and continuous refinement in silence.

Criteria for Advancement

  • Consistency of daily Sādhana
  • Observance of Guru-āgya
  • Emotional & Psychological stability
  • Containment of experience
☸

The Role of the Guru

The Guru determines timing. The disciple does not self-initiate. The Guru observes stability and confers advancement accordingly. Krama unfolds under guidance — not self-direction.

"Readiness is recognised — not requested."

The Spirit of Progression

Sādhana is not accumulation of initiations. It is refinement of consciousness. The disciple who advances steadily becomes inwardly quiet, develops clarity, reduces reactivity, and deepens devotion.

The Process

DIKSHA PROCESS

Whether online or in person, the integrity of initiation remains rooted in lineage, discipline, and preparedness.

💻

Online Diksha

  • Pre-Diksha Preparation Specific fasting, lifestyle instructions, and space preparation.
  • Scheduled Session Conducted via secure platform with formal initiation moment.
  • Post-Diksha Protocol Written instructions and daily practice guidelines provided.

"Online Diksha demands the same seriousness as physical presence."

⚡

After Diksha

Diksha marks responsibility. After initiation, the disciple must:

  • ● Maintain uninterrupted daily Sādhana
  • ● Observe strict confidentiality
  • ● Report difficulties honestly
  • ● Avoid modification of instructions

"Transmission strengthens through practice."
