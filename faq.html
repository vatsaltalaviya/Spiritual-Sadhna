Clarity & Understanding

FREQUENTLY
ASKED QUESTIONS

"This page addresses common questions from aspirants considering entry into a disciplined Sadhana Parampara. The responses are intended for sincere seekers who approach Sādhana with responsibility."

1. Who is eligible to apply for Diksha?

+

Eligibility depends on sincerity, psychological stability, and willingness to maintain disciplined daily Sādhana. This Parampara is suitable for serious aspirants prepared for structured progression, not for casual experimentation. A preparatory period is mandatory before formal initiation.

2. Who should not apply?

+

Those seeking quick siddhis, dramatic experiences, or spiritual status are not aligned with this tradition. Individuals unwilling to maintain daily discipline, observe maryādā, or follow Guru-āgya should refrain from applying. Sādhana requires steadiness, not curiosity.

3. Can I receive Diksha if I already have a Guru?

+

If you are firmly established in another Guru–Śiṣya relationship, you are encouraged to continue there.

Entering a second Parampara without clarity creates inner conflict. Transparency regarding prior initiation is required during application.

4. Can I mix practices from multiple traditions?

+

This Parampara emphasises containment and focused progression. Mixing multiple mantra systems or ritual structures without guidance may create energetic and psychological imbalance.

5. Is online Diksha valid?

+

Transmission depends upon alignment, readiness, and Guru guidance — not on physical proximity. Online or offline, all dikshas depend on the transmitter (guru) and the receiver (shishya).

6. Can I take diksha from other places while continuing here?

+

No. HSGP does not advise mixing of sadhanas, initiations and guru paramparas.

7. How long does progression take?

+

There is no fixed timeline. Advancement depends upon:

  • ● Consistency of daily practice
  • ● Emotional stability
  • ● Observance of Guru-āgya
  • ● Ethical alignment

Krama unfolds according to readiness, not demand.

8. Is this suitable for householders?

+

Yes. HSGP recognises the integration of Gṛhastha dharma and Sādhana. Spiritual refinement does not require withdrawal from responsibility. Discipline within daily life is considered strength, not limitation.

9. What if I miss daily practice?

+

Occasional unavoidable interruptions are understood. However, habitual inconsistency indicates lack of readiness for advanced stages. Continuity is essential for stable transmission.

10. Is Tantra dangerous?

+

Tantra approached without discipline can destabilise. Tantra approached within Parampara, under Guru guidance, is structured, progressive, and protective. Krama exists to ensure safety.

11. What is expected after Diksha?

+
  • Maintain daily Sādhana
  • Preserve confidentiality
  • Follow instruction precisely
  • Uphold Dharma in conduct

"Diksha is beginning — not conclusion."

12. Spiritual experiences guaranteed?

+

Experiences are not the measure of progress. Stability, clarity, humility, and reduction of inner reactivity are stronger indicators of advancement. Depth develops gradually.

13. Ultimate aim of this Parampara?

+

The aim is disciplined refinement of consciousness through mantra, tantra, and structured sadhana progression. The goal is alignment with Dharma and realisation of the deeper ground of awareness — not display of power.

14. How do I begin?

+

Begin with the Preparatory Period. Stability precedes initiation. When readiness is demonstrated, Diksha may be conferred.
