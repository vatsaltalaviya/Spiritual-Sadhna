Siddhashram

The Himalayan
Siddhashram Tradition

The Himalayan Siddhashram Guru Parampara stands within the ancient Guru–Śhiṣya tradition of Sanātana Dharma — a living current of transmission flowing through realised masters and disciplined disciples.

This Parampara traces its continuity through Paramhans Swami Sachchidanand Ji and Paramhans Swami Nikhileshwaranand Ji, and a miniscule attempt to continue it is being made by Brahmanshi as a living channel of that lineage. Transmission flows through initiation (Diksha), daily practice, maryādā, and Guru-āgya.

Paths

The Path of Sādhana

Within this Parampara, Sādhana unfolds progressively. It integrates precise sound transmission, disciplined ritual science, and sacred geometry.

Mantra

Precise sound transmission for inner purification and awakening. A bridge to the divine frequency.

Tantra

Disciplined ritual science aligning body, breath, and subtle energies in alignment with universal flow.

Yantra

Sacred geometry as inner cosmology — especially through Śrīcakra upāsanā, a map of the absolute.

Śrī Vidyā — Krāmik Dīkṣā

Ordered initiation including Mahā Gaṇapati, Guru Pādukā, Bālā Tripurasundarī, and the higher Navāvaraṇa Pūjan.
